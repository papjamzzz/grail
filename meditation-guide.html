01 · System Model

The field is your nervous system

Each particle maps to a biomarker data point — HRV, cortisol, respiratory rate, resting heart rate, recovery. Eight rings, each corresponding to how directly that system responds to meditation. Inner rings = highest sensitivity. Outer rings = slower, cumulative change.

03 · Output Signals

Reading the biomarker panel

The five values on the right are estimated effect sizes derived from peer-reviewed literature on meditation and autonomic physiology. They are weighted by each marker's known sensitivity to practice — HRV at 1.0×, cortisol at 0.87×, resting HR at 0.76×, respiratory rate at 0.62×, recovery at 0.56×.

HRV and cortisol move fastest. Resting HR follows slowly over weeks. Respiratory rate shifts within a single session. Recovery is the composite output — it rises as the others move.

Hover any marker on the panel to see what it measures and what the clinical evidence says about meditation's effect on it.

04 · Places to Practice

Where to go if you want to go deeper

These are real places, real teachers. Not a curated list — just the ones worth knowing about.

Dhamma Dhara — Vipassana Meditation Center
Shelburne Falls, Massachusetts
The first Vipassana center established outside India, founded in 1982 under S.N. Goenka. The standard course is ten days of silence — no reading, writing, or devices. Capacity for 200 students, 15–20 courses per year, consistently full with a waitlist. Donation-based, open only to those who have completed a prior course. The origin point of Goenka Vipassana in the West.
dhara.dhamma.org
Insight Meditation Society
Barre, Massachusetts
One of the preeminent Vipassana retreat centers in the Western world. Residential retreats from three days to three months. The teachers here trained most of the teachers everywhere else.
dharma.org
Spirit Rock Meditation Center
Woodacre, California
The West Coast counterpart to IMS. Beautiful land, serious practice, excellent teacher lineup. Daylong programs and longer residential retreats. Strong community.
spiritrock.org
Insight Meditation Community of Washington
Washington, D.C.
An active urban sangha with weekly sits, study groups, and retreats. If you are near DC and looking for community practice, this is the place. Tara Brach leads here.
imcw.org
Transcendental Meditation
Worldwide
The most clinically studied meditation technique in existence. The twice-daily protocol — 20 minutes morning and evening — is what drives most of the cortisol and blood pressure research. Requires in-person instruction.
tm.org
Against the Stream
Online and in community
Founded by Noah Levine. A more contemporary, accessible entry into Insight Meditation — no prerequisites, no gatekeeping. Good for people who are early in practice and want direct instruction without the traditional structure.
againstthestream.org
