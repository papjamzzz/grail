How It Works

The field is your nervous system

Each particle in the visualization represents a biomarker data point — heart rate variability, cortisol, respiratory rate, resting heart rate, and recovery. They orbit in rings corresponding to how directly each marker responds to meditation practice.

When you select a practice level, the particles change. At no practice, the field is chaotic — particles fly and scatter. As you commit more deeply, the field slows, organizes, and begins to form coherent patterns. At the deepest levels, a double helix emerges.

This is not decoration. It is a biological metaphor. The body under stress is incoherent — systems firing without rhythm. The body in deep practice is ordered, slow, and self-regulating.

The Right Panel

What the percentages mean

The five biomarkers on the right show the estimated effect size of your practice level on each marker, based on peer-reviewed literature on meditation and physiology. These are directional guides, not clinical predictions.

HRV and cortisol are the most sensitive to practice. Resting heart rate follows slowly. Respiratory rate shifts almost immediately. Recovery is the composite — when the others move, it moves with them.

Hover any marker to read what it measures and what the evidence says about meditation's effect on it.

Places to Practice

Where to go if you want to go deeper

These are real places, real teachers. Not a curated list — just the ones worth knowing about.

Dhamma Dhara — Vipassana Meditation Center
Shelburne Falls, Massachusetts
The first Vipassana center established outside India, and the first in the Western Hemisphere. Founded in 1982 under S.N. Goenka, in the tradition of Sayagyi U Ba Khin. The standard course is ten days of silence — no reading, no writing, no devices. A meditation hall for 200 students. Fifteen to twenty courses per year, nearly every one full with a waitlist. All courses run on donation only, accepted solely from those who have already completed a course. This is the origin point of Goenka Vipassana in the West.
dhara.dhamma.org
Insight Meditation Society
Barre, Massachusetts
One of the preeminent Vipassana retreat centers in the Western world. Residential retreats from three days to three months. The teachers here trained most of the teachers everywhere else.
dharma.org
Spirit Rock Meditation Center
Woodacre, California
The West Coast counterpart to IMS. Beautiful land, serious practice, excellent teacher lineup. Daylong programs and longer residential retreats. Strong community.
spiritrock.org
Insight Meditation Community of Washington
Washington, D.C.
An active urban sangha with weekly sits, study groups, and retreats. If you are near DC and looking for community practice, this is the place. Tara Brach leads here.
imcw.org
Transcendental Meditation
Worldwide
The most clinically studied meditation technique in existence. The twice-daily protocol — 20 minutes morning and evening — is what drives most of the cortisol and blood pressure research. Requires in-person instruction.
tm.org
Against the Stream
Online and in community
Founded by Noah Levine. A more contemporary, accessible entry into Insight Meditation — no prerequisites, no gatekeeping. Good for people who are early in practice and want direct instruction without the traditional structure.
againstthestream.org
