HRV —
Heart Rate Variability
The gap between heartbeats, measured in milliseconds. Higher variability means your nervous system is adaptive and resilient. Low HRV is one of the clearest signs of chronic stress.
Meditation is the most evidence-backed intervention for HRV. Even 20 minutes daily produces measurable improvement within two weeks.
Resting HR —
Resting Heart Rate
Your heart rate at rest. Lower is calmer. Athletes sit at 40–50 bpm. Most adults run 60–80. Elevated resting HR is a direct signal of sympathetic overdrive.
Regular meditation activates the parasympathetic system. Most practitioners see resting HR drop 3–5 bpm within a month of consistent practice.
Cortisol —
Cortisol
Your primary stress hormone. Peaks in the morning, should taper through the day. Chronically elevated cortisol disrupts sleep, immunity, metabolism, and mood.
Meditation reduces cortisol more reliably than almost any other non-pharmaceutical intervention. TM studies show 30–32% reduction with consistent twice-daily practice.
Resp Rate —
Respiratory Rate
Breaths per minute at rest. Normal is 12–20. Slower, deeper breathing is directly correlated with calm nervous system function and better oxygen efficiency.
The most immediate effect of meditation. Breath slows within minutes of sitting. Over time, your resting rate shifts lower as the practice becomes the baseline.
Recovery —
Recovery Index
A composite score drawn from HRV, resting HR, sleep quality, and respiratory rate. It measures how well your body bounces back. Declines with poor sleep, stress, and overtraining.
As the primary markers improve through meditation, recovery rises with them. It is the downstream signal. If you are practicing, this number moves.
