How to read your Night
Last night's sleep, drawn as a landscape — from lights-out on the left to waking on the right.
The terrain is your sleep architecture. Bright cyan ridges are REM — the mind dreaming and filing memory. Purple valleys are deep sleep — the body's repair. Blue hills are light sleep between them.
Amber spikes are brief awakenings. Everyone has them — most you'll never remember.
The traveler is you, crossing the night. It replays once like a film with the story below — then the night is yours to drag and explore.
The ember line is your heart rate sinking through the night — lowest in the deepest hours.
The green line is HRV climbing as you recover — it peaks in the deepest valley.
The moon hangs over the deepest hour of your night.
