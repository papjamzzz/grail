How to read your Ring
Your whole day on one clock — midnight at the top, noon at the bottom.
The bright cursor is right now. The ring fills in behind it as your day is written — everything ahead is still blank. The center shows the time, state, and HR & HRV at the cursor.
Drag anywhere on the ring to look back through your day. Let go and the cursor glides home to now.
◐
The ◐ button up top switches views: Today so far (default), or the rolling last 24 hours — where the dimmed stretch ahead of the cursor is yesterday closing the circle.
The blue arc is sleep. Purple stretches inside it are deep sleep; the cyan dashes just inside are REM.
Hot pink flares are training. Yellow arcs are lighter movement, like a walk.
The red line outside the ring is your heart rate — watch it swell where you trained and sink while you slept.
The cyan line inside is HRV — highest in deep night, dipping after hard effort, rebounding as you recover.
The ring's daytime color is your stress load — green is calm, amber is working, red is a spike worth noticing.
Amber trails are caffeine burning off — each dose fades over ~10 hours. If a trail reaches your sleep arc, that espresso is still in your blood at bedtime.
